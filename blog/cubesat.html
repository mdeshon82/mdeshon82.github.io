(and actually launching it)

Here’s the thesis, loud and clear:

With a little will, some ingenuity, and a credit card, literally anyone can build and launch a homemade satellite into orbit in 2026 without a six-figure budget or a government grant.

I’m doing it right now. This is the real-time build log.

The Goal

A fully deployer-compatible 1U CubeSat that:

  • Deploys a nitinol tape-spring antenna 30 minutes after ejection
  • Collects temperature, voltage, gyro, and GPS fix
  • Takes picutures when requested from grounnd station
  • Beacons everything on 70 cm amateur band every 60 seconds, or when requested from ground station
  • Survives launch on a rideshare, or maybe homemade rocket hmmmmm.........

Target cost for the satellite itself: under $2,000. Launch slot: another ~$15k–$30k via aggregators (still cheaper than a new car).

Core Philosophy: The Satellite Is Just a Space PLC

That’s not a joke. I’m treating the entire bird like an industrial controller floating in vacuum:

  • One central brain (STM32F411RE “Black Pill”)
  • Modular I/O (sensors, radio, power)
  • Watchdog timers everywhere
  • Relentless ground testing until it’s bulletproof

If a $300 Allen-Bradley Micro820 can run a factory 24/7, a $7 STM32 can survive orbit.

Parts List – Almost Entirely Amazon Prime

  • Structure: 6061 square tubing + 3D-printed corners (~$60)
  • Brain: STM32F411RE Black Pill (100 MHz ARM Cortex-M4, 512 KB flash) (~$7)
  • Power: 4× 18650 cells, TP4056 chargers, 5 V flexible solar panels, tiny MPPT (~$90)
  • Radio: Dorji DRF4463D20 Si4463 module + nitinol deployable dipole (~$45)
  • Sensors: BMP280, ICM-20948 9-DOF, OV2640 camera module (~$40)
  • Deployment: Nichrome burn-wire + spring-loaded antenna canister (~$30)

Grand total for flight hardware: ~$350

Project Timeline – Breadboard to Orbit

Phase 1: Prototype (Now – Feb 2026)

  • STM32 flight software in C (FreeRTOS + watchdog hell)
  • Burn-wire + antenna deployment on the bench
  • First “hello world” packets received on RTL-SDR

Phase 2: Full Stack-Up (Mar – Jun 2026)

  • Machine aluminum rails
  • Custom PCBs for power and sensor breakout
  • 24-hour thermal stress test in the freezer/toaster-oven rig

All that’s left is someone stubborn enough to solder it together and click “Buy Now” on a launch slot.
